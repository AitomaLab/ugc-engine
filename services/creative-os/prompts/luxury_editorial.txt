• Each prompt must be:
  - One single paragraph

  - Realistic and professional

1. SUBJECT & PRESENCE (EDITORIAL RULE)

Describe the subject with editorial restraint and authority:
- Calm posture
- Natural elegance
- Controlled body language
- Subtle facial expression (never exaggerated)
- Fashion-forward attitude
- Quiet confidence

If a model:
- Emphasize bone structure, posture, neck, shoulders, hands
- No over-posing
- No exaggerated emotion

If a product:
- Treated as an object of desire
- Sculptural presence
- Clean silhouette
- Perfect proportions

Everything must feel intentional, composed, and timeless.

2. CAMERA & FRAMING (HIGH FASHION STANDARD)

Define framing with precision:
- Editorial focal length feel (35mm–50mm for models, 80mm for product shots)
- Clean composition
- Straight horizons
- Balanced negative space
- No distortion
- Natural perspective

Aesthetic keywords allowed:
"editorial framing, refined composition, professional fashion photography, clean depth of field, premium optical rendering"

❌ Never mention camera brands, models, or equipment explicitly.

3. LIGHTING (CRITICAL RULE)

Lighting must always feel professional and intentional:
- Soft directional key light
- Controlled contrast
- Gentle shadow falloff
- Natural highlights (never blown)
- Subtle sculpting of face or product
- Clean separation from background

Indoor:
- Window-style light
- Soft bounce
- Editorial studio feel

Outdoor:
- Natural daylight shaped by environment
- Overcast, golden hour, or controlled sun
- No harsh uncontrolled light

❌ Never use dramatic cinematic lighting
❌ Never use artificial or flashy effects

4. ENVIRONMENT & BACKGROUND

The environment must feel editorial, intentional, and concept-driven — not empty or generic.

Locations should support the narrative and identity of the product or model, reflecting mood, status, and brand positioning while remaining visually controlled.

Allowed environments include:
- Refined architectural spaces (modern, brutalist, classical, or contemporary)
- Natural landscapes with character and atmosphere (not postcard-perfect)
- Curated interiors with depth, texture, and material presence
- Editorial locations that add context, emotion, or storytelling without visual chaos

The background must:
- Enhance the subject's presence and meaning
- Never distract or overpower the subject
- Remain visually composed and intentional
- Feel lived-in or elevated, not sterile nor cluttered

Minimalism is a choice, not a rule.
Narrative, atmosphere, and visual intention always come first.

5. COLOR GRADING (HIGH FASHION ONLY)

Color grading must be editorial and timeless, never trendy or aggressive.

Choose one naturally:
- Soft neutral palette
- Warm ivory, stone, sand, taupe, charcoal
- Natural skin tones
- Slightly muted saturation
- Fine, almost imperceptible film grain

Avoid:
❌ Teal & orange
❌ Heavy contrast
❌ HDR looks
❌ Over-stylization

Keywords allowed:
"editorial color grading, soft tonal transitions, natural skin tones, refined contrast, luxury magazine finish"

6. MOVEMENT (ONLY IF NEEDED)

If movement is present:
- Natural fabric motion
- Subtle walking motion
- Wind in hair or garment
- Soft motion blur only if realistic

Otherwise:
- Stillness is preferred
- Calm, statuesque presence
